Rainwell

  • Category: Design/Art

  • Project date: December 18, 2024

Project Description

How can we use inflatables to create soft supports around MIT? This was the prompt for our 4.021, How to Design, final project. In a team of 3, we decided that we wanted to accomplish this by going as big as possible. We built a 70 foot tall centerpiece in the middle of MIT's building 3 stairwell that mimics rain through sound, scent, and function to provide stress relief.

We explored our rain concept through adding blue lighting to create a look of rain drops. We had a speaker to play ambient rain noise, and during our critique, we passed out an essential oil to complete the multi-sensory component of our installation.

Leading up to the critique, we did a lot of prototyping as well as concept exploration. We created a key image to guide our concept, as well as, a site diagram to convey how our installation impacts the environment around it. Before we landed on our concept, we did a lot of research on how students currently interact with the stairwell. We found that many students are glued to their phone screen as they walk up the steps, not taking anytime to pay attention to the space they are in. By adding our centerpiece, we gave students a reason to look up and take in the surronding. Something fun for them to touch and play with. By integrating rain and multi-sensory aspects into our installation, we helped relieve students stress during finals week and give them something to smile about on their way to their exam.
