Project Description

This was my first big 4.021, How to Design, project. I learned a lot about the design process through trying to understand what makes things creepy, eerie, etc. This project came about a fascination with the uncanny valley effect, which is a creepy sensation primarily assocated with robots that look like humans. I wanted to get away from the ties with AI and humans, so I choose to explore more generally eerie movement in botanical models. I was inspired by looking at pictures of strange plants that often evoke similar feelings of unsettlingness.

I started this process by prototyping with simple paper structures and trying to understand what makes movement eerie. I tested by moving them with my hand and then I advanced to exploring with small motors. Ultimately, I landed on using small fans to drive slow enough movement to align with the eerieness. I also played a lot with color in the final construction since I saw that in a lot of the strange plants. I am not entirely sure how effective the color was which is why I kept the main photos pictured in black and white.

I learned a lot about the design process in this project, specifically how to iterate, ideate, and prototype quickly.
