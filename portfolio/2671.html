Project Description

Measurement and Instrumentation (2.671) is the one of two required capstone classes for mechanical engineers at MIT. In this class, not only do we learn about experimental techniques for observation and measurement of physical variables such as force, strain, temperature, flow rate, and acceleration, we also conduct our own research projects throughout the semester which culminate in a final poster and paper. I decided to study knife throwing, which is a hobby that I've picked up through my living community. The goal of knife throwing is to stick the knife into the target, and I wanted to answer the question of what exactly makes the knife stick?

I measured my friend throwing a knife at a force plate 45 times and then analyzed it in Vernier Video analysis. I identified 3 main conditions that increase the sticking probability of the knife, which were an optimal range in total flight angle, impulse, and final angle.
