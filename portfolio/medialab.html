Media Lab Research

  • Category: Research
  • Tools Used: Data Analysis, Processing.js, Python, Javascript, React.js
  • Project date: Jan 2023-May 2024

Project Description

From the start of my freshman spring to the end of my sophmore spring, I worked as an undergraduate researcher in the Media Lab. During this time I worked in both the Affective Computing and Personal Robotics Group including about 4 different projects.

I started working in the Affecive Computing Group under Matthew Groh, as he was wrapping up his PhD, studying the potential of large language models expressing empathy. I worked on integrating the ChatGPT API into an empathetic agent within a wordle game. This work was expanding off a study that indicated empathetic agents reduce frustration during game play. I presented my work at the 2023 Media Lab Sponsors Conference.

During the following summer, I started working with Ila Kumar in Affective Computing and Jocelyn Shen in Personal Robotics. On Ila's project, I worked on quick prototyping software apps for helping foster youth well-being. I studied the integration of AI tools, such as Dall-e, to design these apps in creative ways. Ultimately, what we learned was that AI is not the best tool for this project. We moved towards developing an app that lets users create and share their own comic stories, so they can share their stories through an online community network. I learned a lot about how to ideate, prototype, and pivot in research during this summer. For Jocelyn, I was helping refine their story-telling app that worked through Jibo, a friendly robot, to prepare the app for a country-wide study. I primarily helped with the aesthetics of the UI, but in the following fall, I played a big role in helping conduct their study.

In the fall of 2023, I learned a lot about research studies, for I was helping both Jocelyn and Ila in this endeavor. For Jocelyn's research, we wanted to investigate how robots could play a role in connecting people together through stories. We shipped Jibos across the countries, and I helped get study participants set up with their Jibo, including trouble-shooting any difficulties, as well as conduct interviews throughout the study for date collection. After the study, I helped analyze the data of both interview and survey results and showcase the results in a paper that was recently accepted for HRI 2025, an ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction. For Ila, I helped conduct workshops with foster youth to test our apps and better understand how we can meet their needs. I also helped integrate Jibos into these workshops to see if their was potential for the robots in Ila's research.

Spring 2023, I transitioned to working on a project with Jocelyn Scheirer, where we researched the potential for wearables to aid emotion regulations among individuals with ASD. I helped program and adapt EmotiBits, a device similar to that of an Arduino, into a wearable that ASD users could wear. I then developed a software program using Python and Processing.js that could read the skin conductance data from the wearable to visualize it creatively in a way that would cater to the user.
