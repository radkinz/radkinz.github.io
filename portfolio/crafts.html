Small Projects

Fort Build

Each fall, my dorm comes together to build a huge wooden fort in our courtyard. I helped with the design and CAD effort in SolidWorks for this upcoming build, contributing to the structural framing across three floors—from joists and cross-bracing to safety railings.

3D Printing

During my UROP, I needed a better way to manage loose screws, zip ties, and miscellaneous tools. So I opened up OnShape, sketched out an idea, and printed a custom desk organizer that now lives permanently next to my robot.

Graphic Design

For awhile now, I have been designing logos and posters using Adobe Illustrator as a hobby. These are various designs that I made for some MIT student groups.

Painting

Painting and other methods of art do not come naturally to me, but I still try to be creative when I can and have fun with it. I recently have been very into clothing alterations, so the first picture shows a jean jacket that I painted the subway surfers logo on the back. To aid my painting efforts, I lasercut a cardboard stencil.

The second picture is a mural that I made of a cat with some groovy lines. I am really into the funky style of art which I tried to recreate. The paint was more watery than expected, which is why it started dripping but I decided to embrace that accident and leave the drips to aid the surreal aesthetic.

Reflection

I love doing small projects when I can to embrace my creativity and remind myself all the different ways that engineering can be fun and stress-free.
